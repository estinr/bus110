Week 13: Dec. 10, 2022

A concept from President Monson’s talk resonated with me a lot. Specifically from his talk, he spoke about today’s leprosy including greed, selfishness, and cruelty to name a few. Remember to make room in the inn for the Savior. Have a constant attitude of gratitude. Even when faced with tribulation, retrospectively be grateful for that experience.

Another concept I liked is from Acton Hero: Larry North. What I took away from this video was that entrepreneurial endeavors are not for everyone. You must be able to take blows to the body most cannot handle. It might not be for you. You will never know until you try something on your list of ideas for a business. Be willing to wade through murky water. Have the courage and determination to trudge on through it. The only way onward is forward and through strife. When coming into contact with a mentor, absorb as much as possible. Make sure you internalize it and put it to good use. Having an attitude of belief is essential because it helps your community in the long run when you know what you are doing. I completely agree with Larry here when he says that failures are just as important as success. When his restaurant went out of business, he learned from that experience and learned to build better trenches or something new and better from that. Remember to learn from it, dust yourself off, and get up and walk.

I am greatly appreciative of what I have absorbed from this class. Even though I am taking this class as an elective, I think I learned a lot more going in with an open mind than I would have if I have not taken this class as an elective. I enjoyed reading and listening to the different entrepreneurs and leaders from the course material. I internalized concepts of entrepreneurialism that I could not have guessed were attributes of an entrepreneur like finding the right person to take you on your hero’s journey or making sure you do some introspection to balance your efforts of work and personal life.
