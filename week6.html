Week 6: Oct. 20, 2022

There are three most important takeaways for me from the study material. The first is self-mastery which has two elements within. The element is to set your sail of moral standards. The second is willpower or the wind that carries your vessel. I believe it is important for me to create self-governing rules, goals, and standards to prepare myself for success and prosperity. I think it is crucial to focus on myself by creating these standards before starting a business and/or a family so I could teach employees and family members by example and direction. Work my way toward discipleship through discipline, dedication, and focus.

The second takeaway is like the first though nonetheless different. It is to make rules for yourself as an entrepreneur. These rules can be anything I want them to be, really. I have a mind for philanthropy so I would make a rule or two with that in mind. I would make a rule about how to treat others including myself, employees, others, and family with kindness. I would make several rules for a buffer for time with my family and work environment. It is highly important to have that balance solidified. Without rules or morals, chaos will reign, and I would be lonely, I don’t want that.

The last takeaway encompasses the first two takeaways. By setting rules for myself, I will progress my way toward a better self. The title of the video is “Loyalty to God and Family”. Part of being loyal to God is being loyal to yourself and your family. The video covers one of my family principles from the material this week which is never time crunch God. Never time crunch your family. Never be too busy to accept a calling in the church. If I am, then I need to rethink my priorities. My future business and or start-ups won’t matter in a thousand years, my greatest legacy will be my family and service to the Lord.
